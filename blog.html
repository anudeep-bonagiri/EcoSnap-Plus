Environmental Science & Recycling

Understanding the impact of waste and how technology can help

The Recycling Crisis

Our planet is facing a growing waste crisis. With electronic waste rising five times faster than documented recycling efforts, and only 22.3% of e-waste being properly collected and recycled, we need innovative solutions to address this challenge.

62 million tonnes
The amount of electronic waste generated globally in 2022 - enough to fill 1.55 million 40-tonne trucks, forming a line around the equator.
Source: Global E-waste Monitor 2024

The waste recycling services market has grown to $62.22 billion in 2024, with an expected growth to $78.43 billion by 2028. This growth is driven by increasing awareness of environmental sustainability and technological innovations in waste management.

Why Our App Matters

Our Vision AI recycling detection app helps address one of the key challenges in recycling: identifying what can and cannot be recycled. Studies show that approximately 76% of recyclables are lost at the household level, highlighting the importance of proper education and tools for consumers.

21%
Only 21% of recyclable material is captured in the US, with the majority lost due to consumer confusion about what can be recycled.
Source: The Recycling Partnership

By helping users identify recyclable items with a simple photo, our app aims to reduce contamination in recycling streams and increase the amount of material that can be properly processed and reused.

Plastic Recycling

Approximately 5 million plastic bottles are discarded in the United States every hour, contributing to 35 billion plastic bottles annually. Recycling AI technology detected over 6 billion PET bottles in 2024, showing the scale of the opportunity.

Source: ScottMax Recycling Statistics, Greyparrot

E-Waste Recycling

The average person in the United States generates around 44 lbs of electronic waste annually. Properly recycling e-waste can recover valuable materials worth billions of dollars and prevent toxic substances from entering the environment.

Source: ScottMax Recycling Statistics

Metal Recycling

Recycling rates for aluminum cans have reached approximately 63.6%, with 105,800 aluminum cans recycled every minute in the United States. AI detection systems found that less than 2.5% of metals end up in residue lines.

Source: ScottMax Recycling Statistics, Greyparrot

Textile Recycling

Americans discard 13 million tons of textiles every year, with certain textiles taking up to 40 years to break down in landfills. Proper identification and recycling of these materials can significantly reduce their environmental impact.

Source: ScottMax Recycling Statistics

The Environmental Impact

In 2018, recycling, composting, and proper waste management saved over 193 million metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent emissions - comparable to taking 42 million cars off the road for a year.

79%
The reduction in carbon emissions when using recycled PET compared to virgin plastic material.
Source: Greyparrot

By using our Vision AI app to improve recycling habits, individuals can contribute to significant environmental benefits, including reduced greenhouse gas emissions, conservation of natural resources, and decreased pollution.

How Our Technology Works

Our Vision AI app uses advanced image recognition technology to identify different types of materials and determine their recyclability. Simply point your camera at an item, tap the screen, and the app will tell you whether it can be recycled and how to properly dispose of it.

The technology is similar to systems that have detected over 40 billion waste objects in recycling facilities worldwide in 2024, but packaged in a consumer-friendly mobile application that anyone can use.

By putting this technology in the hands of consumers, we aim to bridge the knowledge gap that leads to recyclable materials being lost at the household level, ultimately increasing recycling rates and reducing environmental impact.
